01 The Problem

Engineering knowledge
is broken.

The institutional knowledge that makes teams move fast doesn't live anywhere a new developer can find it. It lives in people's heads, buried in Slack threads, and scattered across stale documentation.

Documentation is outdated the moment it's written
Wikis and READMEs drift from reality. By the time a new engineer finds them, they're wrong. No one has time to keep them current.
Critical context lives in Slack and disappears
The architecture decision that explains why your codebase looks the way it does? It's in a Slack thread from 2022. Search can't find it. No one remembers the channel.
New developers take 3–4 weeks to become productive
Every hire costs $15k–30k in lost productivity during onboarding. They interrupt senior engineers constantly, compounding the damage.
Senior engineers become human search engines
Your best people spend 30–40% of their time answering the same questions. "Who owns this service?" "Why does this work this way?" "Where is X configured?"

04 Capabilities

Built for engineering teams
that move fast.

Living Knowledge Graph
A Neo4j-powered graph that connects code, documentation, people, and decisions. It doesn't just store information — it understands relationships. Every commit updates the graph. Every PR review is captured. The graph learns as your team works.
Codebase Intelligence
AST-level parsing understands your code structure, not just text. Functions, classes, dependencies, and call graphs — all mapped and queryable in natural language.
Expert Discovery
Ask who built this, who last changed it, who knows it best. Prism scores expertise from actual contribution history — not self-reported org charts.
Personalized Learning Paths
Every new developer gets a curated onboarding journey based on their role, team, and what they'll actually be working on. Not generic. Not overwhelming.
Continuous Learning
The graph doesn't just answer questions — it tracks gaps. When a service has low documentation coverage or concentrated expertise, Prism surfaces the risk proactively.
